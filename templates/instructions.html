How to Connect to Misty II

Follow these steps to connect your laptop to the Misty II robot over WiFi.

ℹ Before you start: Your laptop and Misty II must be connected to the same WiFi network. Misty communicates over your local network — this will not work if they are on different networks.

Setup Steps

1

Power on Misty II

Press and hold the power button on Misty's back until you hear a startup sound and her eyes light up. Give her about 30–60 seconds to fully boot.

The chest LED will glow white when she is ready.

2

Connect Misty to WiFi

If this is your first time, you need to connect Misty to your WiFi network using the Misty App (available on iOS and Android).

Open the app → tap Set Up Robot → follow the on-screen steps to join your WiFi. You only need to do this once.

⚠ Use the same WiFi network that your laptop is on. Misty does not support 5 GHz-only networks — use 2.4 GHz or a dual-band network.
3

Find Misty's IP Address

You need Misty's IP address to connect the dashboard. There are three ways to find it:

📱

Misty App (easiest)

Open the Misty App, select your robot — her IP is shown at the top of the robot detail screen.

🌐

Your Router

Open your router's admin page (try 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1) and find Misty in the connected devices or DHCP list.

💻

Network Scanner

Use a free app like Fing (iOS/Android) or Advanced IP Scanner (Windows) to scan your network and find Misty by name.

The IP will look something like 192.168.1.42 or 10.0.0.25.

4

Enter the IP in the Dashboard

Go back to the Dashboard, type Misty's IP address into the field in the top bar, and click Connect.

The status dot will turn green and you'll see a confirmation in the event log when the connection is successful.

✓ You're connected! Use the panels to drive Misty, move her head and arms, change her expressions, make her speak, and more.

Troubleshooting

"Cannot reach robot. Check IP and WiFi."
Your laptop can't find Misty at that IP. Check that: (1) the IP address is correct, (2) both devices are on the same WiFi network, (3) Misty is fully booted (chest LED is solid white), and (4) no firewall is blocking local network traffic.
"Connection timed out."
Misty is reachable but not responding in time. She may still be booting — wait 30 seconds and try again. If the problem persists, try restarting Misty using the power button.
Connected but commands have no effect
Misty may have a running skill that is blocking commands. Open the Misty App, go to Skills, and stop any running skills. Then retry.
Camera feed is black or not loading
The camera feed requires a stable WiFi connection. Make sure Misty is within range of your router. If her camera is disabled by a running skill, stop it first via the Misty App.
Dashboard won't open / localhost:5000 not found
The Python server isn't running. Double-click start.bat (Windows) or run ./start.sh (Mac/Linux) in the project folder to start it.
Python not found on Windows
Download Python 3.8+ from python.org/downloads. During installation, make sure to check "Add Python to PATH" before clicking Install. Then re-run start.bat.
